who would've thought those had meanings? (he finally figured it out)

Chinen opened his door and got an armful of Nakajima whom he was sure was being held up only by muscle memory and miracles.

"Yuri!" he cheered, throwing his hands up and engulfing the smaller man in an unwarranted hug, the smell of alcohol on his breath not doing much to deter the other who was more than used to it by now.

"Nakajima," Yuri only acknowledges, thankful he has been working out because good heavens, Yuto was heavy.

"Aw, don't be like that, cutie pie," the man pouted, leaning back to squint at him and pinch his cheek. "I was dying all night to see you!"

"I was not," Yuri murmured, half dragging the taller man, half pulling him upright so his pretty face doesn't hit the carpeted hotel floor. "Why aren't you pestering Yamada like you always do?"

 

The tall man and his best friend have had a really good thing going lately - even he noticed that - but he and Yamada have not talked about that yet. He was sure though he would know if something beyond friendship was happening between the two even without the talk but it's still better to tread carefully in case there are fresh boundaries he must not cross.

But does that really matter when the tall man himself came here to HIS room and not Yamada's? Fully aware it was him?

Chinen shook his head and focused on what's in front of him.

 

"Because I wanted to see you," the man pouted further, almost comically, that Yuri would've chuckled if not for the fact that he's annoyed to have been saddled with the responsibility of looking after this giant in his room.

"Right..." Yuri decided to trail off, leaving the man alone on the loveseat near his bed before getting beneath the sheets himself to try to get some rest.

 

He should be mad at him for even thinking he could keep up with Takaki and Inoo while drinking. Those two drank like old men and never got drunk while the rest of them drank a glass and never expected to walk straight after. Being dragged into drinking with them most absolutely ensured a drunk man well beyond the reach of clarity and sense.

Which Nakajima Yuto embodied right now.

"Did you know I got here all by myself?" Nakajima spoke, slumped on the armrest of the chair, raising a hand to the air to gesture. "Inoo-chan said they could take me to my room but I sneaked to see you!"

 

Chinen was tired. He wants to sleep and dream of good things and wake up refreshed. He doesn't want drunk men in his hotel room telling him they sneaked to see him as if expecting him to be grateful.

"And that should flatter me how?" he decided to answer from where he was comfortably tucked in, already regretting the words the moment they came out.

"I came HERE! I wanted to see YOU!" Nakajima exclaimed as if that answered a lot of things - maybe even the most unanswerable question in the universe. Well, in his drunken brain, it made a lot of sense. "I crossed the oceans for you!"

"I would hardly call a hallway an ocean but do go on. Profess your undying love," Yuri sighed, finally giving up and just sitting so he could keep an eye on Nakajima in case he finally decides to succumb to sweet sleep and kiss that floor with his entire face.

"Oh I will," the taller man said daringly, moving so that he was more upright and facing Chinen's way.





By the end of his speech, Chinen was red in the face, almost on the verge of rolling over and begging the floor to swallow him whole because Nakajima definitely knew how to use words to express what he was thinking. He was sober enough to recount in detail the way Yuri's hair fell just right over his eyes that when he looked up at him, he felt more than tempted to lean down and devour his lips in a heated kiss that would stay with him for days - how he has to hold himself like a man holding back a famish lion from devouring Chinen Yuri. He was definitely coherent enough to tell Yuri what he thought exactly of his arms and his hands and his lips - boy does the man have a fixation on his lips. All the ways he would kiss him and in places he'd imagined doing it too -

Who knew a man like him actually had the capacity to think that way.

"I wouldn't," the other man agreed so seriously Chinen had to lift his head and see if the man was still slumped on the chair drunk with how sober he sounded

"But you can make it memorable."





Chinen did not make it memorable. Instead, he turned and watched Yuto struggle on the loveseat before pitying him and moving him to the space next to him in bed. The man was so smashed he couldn't even move his legs to take advantage of the freely given situation.

"Yuri," he whined, lifting an arm up to reach him to no avail. "Baby please hug me. I'm cold."

"Do you want me to warm you up?" Chinen deadpanned, amused when the drunken man grinned, nodding vigorously despite his sorry state.

 

Instead of whatever that question promised, the blanket was only raised until it was tucked beneath Yuto's chin and he was pouting, whining softly as he tried to fight exhaustion.

"If you behave, I promise to reward your efforts," Yuri almost purred and that got Nakajima to sit still for once.

But that also lead to him falling asleep and it was more amusing than it should've been but Yuri was already tired and would really like that sleep now, drunk man next to him or not.





Yuri woke up earlier than the other and he relished the fact that he could mess up with him better this way. Just to retaliate, of course, for ruining his night by barging in drunk.

He snuggled up to him and woke him up by lightly tracing random patterns on the man's skin. When felt the man take a sharp breath in, he sighed, as if in contentment, and hummed something pleased. He's not just a singer, he's also an actor. And a damn good one.

"What...?" He heard Yuto breathe before reaching a hesitant arm to wrap around him while he lazily laid on his chest, still purring.

"Morning, sleepy head," Yuri rasped, voice just scratchy enough that it hinted at a lot of things that could be conceived by the more imaginative. "You wore me out last night."

"I... did?" Yuto asked, sounding more confused than cocky and really, he knows why but he'll try to prolong the fun.

"Mhmm," Yuri nodded, still making those senseless patterns, only now they were more suggestive than before. "I'm ruined for others - "

He'll just have to talk to Yama-chan first thing later and tell him what happened so he knows Yuto's not cheating if they are indeed actually together at the moment.

He won't have to reveal that he can't really remember how he even ended up in Chinen's room in the first place when he could only clearly remember Inoo offering to walk him to his room. He'll have to rely on Yuri's cues to see where this was going.

 

But Yuri was going to lead him nowhere. There was nowhere to go. But it was too fun and he just -

"Time to get up. Work day ahead," Yuri sighed as if detaching himself from his warmth was truly a loss. He even leaned down to leave a lingering kiss on his forehead - like a promise that they'll pick up where they left off as soon as they could.

Only there was nothing to pick up because there was nowhere they left off.

But Yuto didn't have to know that just yet.

 

Making his way to the bathroom, he suggestively stretched his back, groaning just right to give him an idea that he aches in all the right places.





"You're a lunatic," Yamada laughed when he got him alone in the cafeteria with Fuma. Fuma didn't really care - he's had his bit of fun with a certain senior we will not be naming - and as long as nobody's talking about him, he ain't talking about anybody else either.

He opened the conversation by asking if there was anything going on between him and Nakajima. When it was a negative, he proceeded to tell him about what transpired the night before and watched his reaction go from worried to surprised to plain disbelieving.

"So that's why he's been staring at you earlier," Yamada said after calming down. "Like he can't decide if he should go to you or stay away from you."

"If he's smart, he'd know what he should do," Yuri shrugged.





Turns out, Nakajima ain't so smart as to have even a clue of what to do. He stayed a safe distance away but he also hovered - he's always around. Everywhere Yuri went, he was there. A few hours into the day, it was amusing. Towards the end, Yuri wanted nothing but to get rid of him ANY way possible.

"That's what you get for making him think something happened," Yamada said reprimandingly and he had every right to do so; this is his fault. He probably should've told him earlier than he was just messing around with him but the day got the better of him and took any more alone time away.

A curse under his breath and he was off, leaving Yamada with Arioka who was on the other side of the room, chatting with Inoo and Takaki.

If Yuri is right about what he wants to say - and were it actually the case - sitting near him would've been the worst option. He's small but he packs a punch.

Nakajima stopped when Yuri snorted, looking every bit in the world trying to stop himself from laughing. "Chii?"

Red. He was red and it looked good on him. Miraculously.

That got him a reaction - Nakajima burying himself on his pillows, saying something unintelligible - or probably he was just saying gibberish. It didn't stop Chinen however from laughing maniacally, joining him on the bed, finally straddling him and really driving a point by pinning him down.

He might not look it but he was strong and Nakajima's answering gasp was proof.

 

And like a switch, it changed the mood.





As if it was only waiting that moment, everything came crashing down on him: the last few months, the flower waiting for him at home, the thinly veiled casualness they have been keeping up since that conversation they had.

Nakajima likes him.

And he likes Nakajima right back.

 

The late realization almost made him want to laugh. How they both managed to last that long without doing anything - not even addressing what's been simmering beneath the surface for the longest time - is absolutely beyond him.

"You're a mad man," Yuri smiled, the hand that was pinning a hand above Yuto's head earlier now gently caressing the cheek of the man who leaned to his touch like he was meant to be doing that. "I wouldn't have lasted half as long waiting on someone."

"Call it a full-time job," Nakajima winked and Yuri couldn't really get mad at him for that.





"I won't be anything you expect me to be," Yuri murmured against skin, the taller man pillowed now on his chest as he breathed him in. "You can't show me around - I won't be showing you around,"

There was a delicate silence between them. Yuri knew that was a little bit too much to ask considering he was willing to give so little. Not to say he lacks experience in relationships but he knows himself better now. It took a lot to get where he is and he's not willing just yet to give it away for a man.

Even if said man is Nakajima Yuto.

Only now, when he glanced Yuri's way, there was that shiver he couldn't hold back - all the ways he was owned last night washing him cold and alert.

"Good morning, Chii," Nakajima greeted him, as if earlier this morning he wasn't devouring him against the wall of his bathroom, making him forget his name for a good few minutes.

"Nakajima," Yuri nodded politely before turning his attention back to his phone, doing everything he could to ignore the taller man who decided it was okay to sit next to him.

"What's up?" he asked no nonchalantly that he was almost tempted to just -

"Nothing much. Just the usual what I should ask Yama-chan to buy me this week," Yuri shrugged and Yuto laughed.

 

That sneaky hand that squeezed his might've made him smile but it was gone as soon as it came.
